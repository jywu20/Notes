\chapter{Noun phrase}

Gyeli has six genders and two numbers recognized.
The features are realized on the noun prefix 
and various agreement targets.%
\footnote{
    Some authors use the term \term{target} to refer to 
    the head noun, 
    since its features are what is \translate{probed} 
    by words that agree with it.
    This note however follows the terminology in \citet{grimm2021grammar}.
} 
The morphological realization of the two agreement categories however 
is highly nontrivial.

There are nine classes of possible varieties of the agreement morphemes
on the aforementioned various agreement targets,
named as agreement classes 1-9;
class 3 and 9 have subclasses \citep[\citetable{5.2}]{grimm2021grammar}.
The agreement classes encode both gender and number,
and the morphological class of the stem as well;
thus it's possible -- and actually almost obligatory -- 
that the single and plural forms of one noun belong to \emph{two} agreement classes
\citep[\citepage{292}]{grimm2021grammar}.
Indeed, this is how the six genders are named:
the gender under which the singular nouns belong to class 1 and 2 
is known as gender 1/2, etc. 
\citep[\citepages{303-304}, \citefig{5.2}]{grimm2021grammar}.

The noun prefix, on the other hand, is partially decided by the agreement class 
(and therefore gender and number of the noun), 
but there is no one-to-one relation between the two:
nouns in classes 7 and 9 never have the prefix,
and the nasal prefix \form{N-} appears for both class 1 and class 3,
so it's not always possible to tell the agreement class from the noun prefix;
on the other hand, 
it's also not always possible to tell the noun prefix from the agreement class, 
because for agreement class 1, 2, 5, 6, 8 
multiple prefixes are possible 
\citep[\citetable{5.4}]{grimm2021grammar}. 

In conclusion, to correctly describe the morphological behavior of a noun, 
we need to record its gender, so that once we know whether it's singular or plural 
we are able to decide its agreement class 
(for classes 3 and 9 additional information is needed); 
we also need to record the correct noun prefix.
